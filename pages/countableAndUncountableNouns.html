Countable and uncountable nouns

Countable nouns are nouns that can be counted and have a plural form. They refer to things that can be quantified as individual units. For example, "dog" is a countable noun, and its plural form is "dogs". You can count how many dogs you see, and you can say "one dog" or "two dogs". Uncountable nouns, on the other hand, are nouns that cannot be counted as individual units and do not have a plural form. They refer to things that are not easily quantifiable. For example, "water" is an uncountable noun. You can't count how many waters you have, and you can't say "two waters". Instead, you would use a different unit of measurement, such as "two glasses of water". For example, "paper" can refer to a sheet of paper (countable) or to the substance made from wood pulp (uncountable). • There are some specific uncountable nouns that have their own rules, such as "advice", "furniture", and "information".
